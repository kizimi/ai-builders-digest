Vocabulary

  • engulfed /ɪnˈɡʌlft/ verb

    completely surrounded or absorbed by something; taken over entirely

    The entire workflow can be engulfed by LLMs with no classical code needed.

    Also: consumed, absorbed, subsumed, swallowed

  • paradigm /ˈpærədaɪm/ noun

    a typical example or pattern of something; a model or framework for understanding concepts

    Every new paradigm change brings both obvious improvements and unexpected possibilities.

    Also: model, framework, pattern, example

  • jaggedness /ˈdʒæɡɪdnəs/ noun

    the quality of being uneven, irregular, or inconsistent; having sharp, rough edges

    The pattern of jaggedness in LLMs explains why they excel at code refactoring but fail at simple tasks.

    Also: unevenness, irregularity, inconsistency

  • verifiability /ˌverɪfaɪəˈbɪləti/ noun

    the quality of being able to be checked, tested, or confirmed as true or accurate

    Domains with high verifiability tend to perform better in LLM training.

    Also: provability, confirmability, testability

  • frontier /frʌnˈtɪr/ noun

    the edge or border of something; the most advanced or cutting-edge area of a field

    Frontier labs choose what to include in training data distributions.

    Also: boundary, edge, forefront, cutting-edge

  • distribution /ˌdɪstrɪˈbjuːʃən/ noun

    the way something is spread out, allocated, or arranged; in ML, the pattern of data used for training

    The training data distribution determines which capabilities the model excels at.

    Also: allocation, spread, arrangement, pattern

  • off-roading /ˌɔf ˈroʊdɪŋ/ verb/noun

    venturing beyond established paths or standard procedures; operating outside normal constraints

    When LLMs venture off-roading in the jungle, they lose coherence and reliability.

    Also: deviating, improvising, ad-libbing

  • refactor /ˌriːˈfæktər/ verb

    to restructure or reorganize code without changing its external behavior; to improve code quality

    An LLM can coherently refactor a 100,000-line codebase.

    Also: restructure, reorganize, optimize

Slang & Jargon

dogfooding

using your own product internally before releasing it publicly; eating your own dog food

on the rails

operating within established parameters or expected behavior; constrained by designed RL circuits

off-roading in the jungle

operating outside trained data distributions; venturing into unpredictable, unverifiable domains

frontier labs

leading-edge AI research laboratories at the cutting edge of LLM development

RL circuits

reinforcement learning circuits; mechanisms that shape model behavior through training signals

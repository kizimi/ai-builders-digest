Vocabulary

  • traction /ˈtrækʃən/ noun

    Real, measurable progress or momentum in a business, demonstrated by concrete metrics like paying customers or revenue

    Free signups are easy to mistake for traction, but real traction comes from paying customers.

    Also: momentum, progress, growth, headway

  • pivot /ˈpɪvət/ verb

    To change business strategy or product direction in response to market feedback or user behavior

    Tibo noticed users hacking the product and decided to pivot the entire product to meet this need.

    Also: shift, redirect, change course, adapt

  • churn /tʃɜːrn/ noun

    The rate at which customers cancel or stop using a product, typically expressed as a monthly percentage

    If monthly churn exceeds 20%, you should focus on product improvement before scaling acquisition.

    Also: attrition, cancellation rate, customer loss

  • tire-kickers /ˈtaɪər ˌkɪkərz/ noun

    People who show interest in a product but have no genuine intention to purchase or commit

    Pricing at $50-100/month is high enough to filter out tire-kickers.

    Also: browsers, window shoppers, looky-loos, prospects without intent

  • MRR /ˌɛm ɑːr ˈɑːr/ noun

    Monthly Recurring Revenue; predictable revenue generated each month from subscription-based customers

    Revid grew from $2K MRR to $600K+ MRR after pivoting to meet user needs.

    Also: recurring revenue, subscription revenue

  • stickiness /ˈstɪkinəs/ noun

    The degree to which users engage with and return to a product over time; user retention quality

    Without revenue and stickiness in revenue, it's very difficult to build a successful business.

    Also: retention, engagement, loyalty, habit formation

  • acquisition /ˌækwɪˈzɪʃən/ noun

    The process of gaining new customers; the cost and effort spent to attract and convert new users

    If churn is too high, stop scaling acquisition and focus on fixing the product first.

    Also: customer acquisition, onboarding, user growth

  • SaaS /sæz/ noun

    Software as a Service; a software distribution model where applications are accessed via subscription over the internet

    Price your AI SaaS at $50-100/month to balance conversion and customer quality.

    Also: subscription software, cloud software, web application

Slang & Jargon

BTS

Behind the Scenes; content showing the hidden or internal workings of a project or business

tire-kickers

Prospects or customers who show casual interest but lack serious purchasing intent

API

Application Programming Interface; a set of protocols allowing different software applications to communicate

MCP

Model Context Protocol; a framework for AI models to interact with external tools and data sources

agents implement

AI agents autonomously executing or building features based on user requests

speedrun

Completing a task or process in the fastest possible time, often with shortcuts or efficiency hacks

pair-agent

A collaborative setup where two AI agents or an AI agent and human work together on a task

name mogging

Using a prestigious or recognizable name to gain credibility or market advantage over competitors

subagents

Smaller AI agents or sub-modules that operate under a parent agent to handle specialized tasks

codex

A comprehensive repository or database of information, code, or knowledge used for reference and retrieval
